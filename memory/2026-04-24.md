Interpreted "95% trial correlation confidence-interval mean" as: for each
anchor stimulus in `full_trajectory`, compute each trial's correlation to the
same-stimulus median prototype across all time-by-neuron features, keep only
trials within the central 95% interval of those correlations, then recompute
the neuron-by-time mean heatmap.

Under that definition, the result changes very little relative to the plain
all-trial mean. Removed only 4 trials per stimulus (`1-Octanol E4`: 48/52 kept;
`Diacetyl E4`: 62/66 kept; `NaCl 150mM`: 62/66 kept). Across all 1755
stimulus-neuron-time cells, trimmed-mean versus all-trial-mean correlation was
`0.9977`, mean absolute cell difference was `0.0146`, median absolute
difference was `0.0073`, and the maximum absolute difference was `0.1761`.
The trimmed mean was not materially closer to the median heatmap than the
plain mean.
