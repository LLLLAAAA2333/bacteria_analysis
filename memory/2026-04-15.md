Reviewed `docs/superpowers/specs/2026-04-15-shared-metabolite-cache-design.md` against the current `model_space_seed.py` implementation. The main conclusion was that the shared cache is intended to preserve curated metabolite identity and taxonomy evidence, decouple Stage 3 from live enrichment, and make outputs reusable across batches. It is not an embedding design. Also noted two design risks: the proposed project-level cache still carries batch-shaped fields like `original_header` and `stage3_metabolite_name`, and the design relies on a notion of "reviewed" rows without fully specifying how that provenance is represented in the canonical cache contract.
